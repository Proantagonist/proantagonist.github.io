Online Safety and Security Guidelines

First things first. Runescape is an online game, which means it’s a lot different from playing a game at home with your friends and family. Even though you might make friends in the game, remember that you DO NOT know these people in real life, unless that is they are real life friends that also play Runescape, but for that you need to know their username and add it to your friends list. It’s important and best to keep a safe distance from other Runescape players.

 

1.NEVER ever give other players your personal information. Don’t tell them your real name, e-mail address, home address, or any phone numbers. If you get a message from someone claiming to work for Runescape, DON’T believe it! Jagex, the company that makes Runescape, will never ask you for this information.

 

2.NEVER ever make plans to meet a Runescape player in real life. There is no way for you to know if the player is really who they say they are, even if they promise or swear. If a player asks to meet you in real life, let a parent or guardian know about it right away.

 

3. Using your game friends list is the safest way to chat with other players, but keep in mind that just because they are in your friends list, it doesn't mean they are to be trusted. If someone makes you feel uncomfortable, STOP chatting right away! You can block all messages from that particular player by clicking on the ignore button on the player interface– a picture with a red unhappy face. You can alo report a player in the game by clicking the report abuse button at the bottom of the game screen.

 

4.Always let a parent or guardian know what you are doing online. Spend some time showing your parents the world of Runescape. Ask them to check on you regularly to see I you're having any problems. And if you do have problem, share it with them.

 

5. Do not give your password to any other player! Period. Don’t tell them your password recovery questions, either. Remember, Jagex staff will never e-mail you and ask you for your password or any other personal info. If you get an e-mail from someone claiming to be from Jagex who wants your personal info, they're lying!

 

6. think before using your password. Never enter your password or recovery questions into ANY Website other than www.runescape.com or www.runescape.co.uk. When you use choose your password, make sure its different from passwords you use on other sites.

 

7. Watch out for fake Web sites! There are websites out there that claim to be official Runescape sites, but they’ve actually been set up to steal your password! Don’t trust a site just because it look official. There are no secret Runescape sites*! The only sites you should log into are www.runescape.com or www.runescape.co.uk.

 

8. Do not use unofficial Runescape add-ons. These are programs that claim to make Runescape easier or better, but they actually steal your password! Besides, using programs like these are against the rules! ( See Rule 7 on “The Rules of Runescape” Section) Most of these programs send your IP address to Jagex so Jagex can ban you.

 

9. Watch out for fake e-mails and fake staff! There are many people out there who pretend to work for Jagex, the company that makes Runescape. Do not trust them. If Jagex need to send a message to Runescape players, they will use the Runescape Message Center.

 

10. Do not share your account with anyone else. Not even you're best friend. First of all, it’s against the rules ( See Rule 6 on “The Rules of Runescape” section).  Its also an easy way to lose your account.
