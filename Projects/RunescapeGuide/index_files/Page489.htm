Using Your Controls Page 3

Friends

You can keep in contact with the other players you make friends with in Runescape by using your Friends List. It’s easy:

 

1. Click on the smiley face on your player interface to call up the Friends Screen List screen.

 

2. To add a friend to the list, click on the button marked ADD FRIENDS and type in the name of the player you want to add. You can also right-click on the name in a player in a Chat window

 

3. To remove a friend from the list, just click on the DEL FRIEND button in the name of the player you want to remove.

 

Ignoring Players

You can also prevent other players from messaging you if you don't want to hear from them anymore:

 

1. Click on the sad-face button on your player interface to call up the Ignore List screen.

 

2. To add a player to the list, click on the button marked ADD NAME and type in the name of the player you want to add. You can also right-click on the name of the player in the chat window and select the correct option.

 

Chat Options

There are for or more options on the bottom of the Runescape screen that control how other players interact with you:

 

1. “Public Chat”: This controls how much chat you can receive from other players. Choose from ON, FRIEND ONLY, OFF, or HIDE.

 

2. “Private Chat”: This controls how players contact you with private messages. If set to ON, anyone can contact you, whether they are a friend or not. You can also choose FRIENDS ONLY and OFF.

 

3. “Trade/Complete”: This controls whether other players can send you trade request or challenge you to duels. The options are ON, FRIENDS ONLY, or OFF.

 

Taking Your Own Screenshots

Want to remember a moment from your game forever? Here’s how you can take screenshots from your game, so you can show your friends your most awesome Runescape accomplishments. Taking screenshots is very easy, but how to do it depends on whether you use Windows or a Macintosh operating system.

 

If You Use Windows:

 

1. Press the PRINT SCREEN on your keyboard. This is located to the right of the F12 key. Pressing this will put whatever is on your screen and send it to your computer’s clipboard.

 

2. Then open up a graphics program such as Paint or Photoshop. Paste your screenshot by pressing CRT+V.

 

3. Save your picture. To make it a smaller file size, try saving it as a .gif or .png file, or cut the Runescape window and paste on another paint window and saving it, this will greatly reduce the file size. If paint is not in the programs menu look at the bottom of this page for instructions.

 

If You Use Macintosh

 

1. Press down the SHIFT + COMMAND (apple) + 3 keys.

 

2. What happens next depends on which Mac system you use. If you use

Mac OS 10.x, a .pdf file with your screenshot in it will be saved to your desktop. You can open it and choose “EXPORT” from the File menu. Then pick a new format to save as. If you use Mac OS 9.x and below, your screenshot will be saved as an image on your desktop.

 

The Members Version of the Game

As you play the game, you may come across a message such as, “You need to be on a members server to use this feature.” Runescape membership requires you to pay a monthly subscription fee of $5. This will give you access to many new game features, including skills such as agility, fletching, herblore, thieving, slayer, farming, hunting, and a brand new skill summoning. It also expands many of the free server skills and also gives access to many new quests, items, towns, dungeons, minigames, and more.

 

This handbook covers the free version of the game, which you can play for as long as you like with no obligation to become a member. But if you do feel you're ready for a new challenge, becoming a member might be for you.

 

 

 

Troubleshooting with finding Paint

 

1. Open my computer, and open your Local Disk. If you’re not sure which one it is, then check which one has the greatest capacity in terms of Gigabytes.

 

2. Once you’ve determined which one it is, double-click on it and open the folder named WINDOWS1 or so.

 

 

 

 

 

3. After that open the folder that says System32.

 

 

 

4. Now look for an application that is named mspaint or mspaint.exe. If you can’t find it arrange your files, by right clicking in an empty space and scroll down to “Arrange Icons By” and select by “Type” and “Show in Groups”

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

5. Now that everything is organized by type look for the section named “Application”, it should be the third section.

 

 

 

6. Now look for mspaint or mspaint.exe as if looking up a word in the dictionary, because they are arranged by alphabetical order.

 

 

 

 

7. Right click on the program and select “Create Shortcut”. Once that’s done drag the shortcut to your desktop, and you're finished! NOTE: The shortcut has a black arrow in the lower left corner, make sure you drag the shortcut not the program.
