Ten Tips For Beginners

 

Know Your Dots.

The little map in the corner of your screen will give you a bird’s-eye view of what's happening in your location. A white dot is a player. A yellow dot is a Runescape character (NCP). And a red dot is an object on the ground that you can pick up.

 

Explore Lumbridge

Do a few things in this village to get used to moving around Runescape.             

 

Use Your Map

As a new player, you will be unfamiliar with your surroundings. That’s what maps are for! Use the one you are given during the tutorial until you can find your way around. You'll be surprised at how helpful a good map can be.

 

Sell Your Stuff

Just like the real world, money makes Runescape go ‘round. You can sell just about anything you catch or make to stores or other players. A good way to practice is to kill chickens in Lumbridge. Chop down a tree, then cook the chicken over a fire. You can eat the cooked chicken, of course, or you can sell it to the general store for 1 GP ( or gold piece). It’s not much, but you can kill and cook more chickens until the GP’s add up, and you'll also get experience( known as XP, or experience points, in Runescape talk).

 

Goblins Are Easy Targets

There are lots of level 2 Goblins running around Lumbridge. If you slay them, they will drop money and other items– which you can pick up.

 

Know Your Specialty Shops

The general store will buy just about anything, but if you try to sell a cooked chicken to Bob’s Brilliant Axes, you’ll be out of luck. If you get an item a specialty shop will buy, you might be able to get a better price for it there than at the general store.

 

Check Prices

You can sell or trade your items to other players in the game, but be sure you don't get ripped off! A good way to check is to see what the item is selling for in stores, then sell your item for close or less. If you are trading, check prices of both items to make sure they're about the same price. You may also find that for certain items, higher–level players will pay even more than shop prices. It’s definitely worth looking out for opportunities like this.

 

More XP Equals More GPs

The more skilled you are at a craft, the more money you can make by selling the things you create by cooking, crafting, mining, or other skills.

 

Beware of the Wilderness

The wilderness used to be a place of player killing , but now, thanks to   an update, that has changed. Although the wilderness is still  dangerous for some lower levels, it is now a much more safer place. If You're in the levels of 30-40 we suggest you try clan wars. Clan wars is a place where you team up with other players to kill another clan (team). Be sure to bring your best amour and weapon, and no need of food or teleportation runes. If you die during the war you lose absolutely nothing, so don't be scared. You will lose your stuff if you get killed by any monster, in the wilderness or not. By the way, the wilderness is located north of Varrock, and if you don't know where   Varrock is, just look at your compass besides your mini map and head all the way northwest.

 

Get to a Bank

If by some chance your character dies( and it probably will), don't freak out. You will end up back in Lumbridge– but any GPs you were carrying will be gone. Keep most of your GPs in a bank and you will   have something to fall back on in times of trouble. You can find a bank    on the top floor of the Lumbridge castle.
