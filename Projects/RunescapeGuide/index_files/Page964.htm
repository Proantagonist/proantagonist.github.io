Mastering Combat

Why Should I Battle?

Let’s face it— the combat option is one of the most popular features on Runescape. It’s a way for ordinary people to unleash their inner warrior in a safe environment. In Runescape, you can slay monsters and battle fierce warriors in competitions of skill and strength.

 

Whether or not you want to fight, it’s a good idea to build up your overall combat level. You never know when a skeleton, scorpion, or some other creature will attack you out of nowhere. It’s good to know how to defend yourself.

 

How Do I Do It?

To fight a monster or character in the game, just point your mouse at your opponent and select the “Attack” option. Not all characters can be attacked, and you can’t attack other players unless you are in the wilderness in any of the minigames, clan wars or bounty hunters. If you’re able to attack a character you’ll see that their combat level is right next to their username.

 

Then it’s time to make a decision. If your opponent's combat level is a lot higher than yours, you probably don't want to mess with him. There are color codes to help you: A weaker opponent will have their combat level colored green, an opponent that is about your level will have either yellow, same level as you, orange, a bit weaken than you, or dark orange, a bit stronger than you, and a opponent that is way stronger than your will have their combat level colored red.

 

If you decide to fight, click on the crossed swords icon on your player interface. This will show what weapons you have and what moves you can do with them. When you choose your move, the battle begins.

 

How Do I Know If I’m Winning or Losing?

Each fighting character will have a horizontal bar above his or her head. The color green means you’re healthy. If you bar starts turning red, it means you are taking damage. If your bar is red, and you opponent’s is still pretty green you can runaway from the battle unless you want to die. Keep moving, because monsters will chase you when you run!

 

What Happens If I Die?

Don’t Panic. If you loose a battle in a battle, your character will “die”. But you won’t be out of the game. You’ll soon end up in Lumbridge, alive and well. You will keep all your stats and skills, but you’ll lose all the items you were carrying, but this will only happen if you die to a monster in or outside the wilderness. If you play the minigame “Clan Wars” in the wilderness, and if you die you wont lose any of your things, and you will respawn in the “Clan Wars” area, well and intact with all you HP restored. If you die in the Duel Arena you wont lose anything unless you’ve staked (bet) an item, plus you'll respawn in  the Duel Arena area with all your HP restored.
